Butterfly’s life course

Life cycle stages

The life cycle of a butterfly is truly amazing. Butterflies have four life stages, the egg, the larva (caterpillar), the pupa (chrysalis), and the adult butterfly. Each of the four stages are very unique to individual species of butterflies which is part of what makes watching and raising butterflies so much fun.

From a tiny egg ...
When an adult female butterfly mates with its male counterpart then she lays eggs which are in the form of oval or cylindrical shape. If you have a closer look at the eggs of a butterfly, then you can even see a caterpillar growing inside the eggs which are not possible to witness in any other animals or insects which lays eggs.
In the second stage of a butterfly lifecycle, a caterpillar comes out of the egg and turn out to be eating machines which have a whole sole motive of eating and eating. An unbelievable fact is that they can even eat food in a quantity greater than their body size. This is the stage when the caterpillar grows and reach to a state which determines the size of the adult butterfly.

The pupa ...
From the catterpillar
The caterpillars have their skin which is known as exoskeleton, which means the caterpillar sheds its outgrown skin several times and grows.Once the caterpillar has grown and taken its maximum size and weight, they transform themselves into a pupa also known as a chrysalis.
When the caterpillar reaches the pupa stage, it is trying to change and transform itself. Inside the pupa, a lot of changes takes place which is mandatory to transform into a grown up or adult butterfly. Caterpillars do not have wings to fly, but when they are in the chrysalis stage, their body parts face transformation which is also known as the metamorphosis stage.
From pupa ...
To a beautiful butterfly
The beautiful butterfly ...
From a pupa
In the metamorphosis stage, the caterpillars get all the beautiful parts of being a butterfly. The wings, the limbs, other butterfly organs, all start developing in this stage to get a complete look of an adult butterfly. After a long wait of three stages, the caterpillar gets transformed into a pretty looking butterfly.
When the butterfly emerges out of the pupa, the wings are folded in an inward fashion towards their bodies and are very soft to touch, these wings gain their own shape after some time. If you see a butterfly coming out of a pupa, then you are surely a lucky person to witness such beautiful creations of God taking its entry into this world.
Searching for a partner to mate ...
So that the circle of life can continue
The life cycle takes its course
After finding the perfect match
Once they start their flight, they search for their partners with whom they can mate and bring more such new butterflies into this world. Male butterflies find females by sight, and use chemicals called pheromones at close range. If the female accepts the male, they couple end to end and may go on a short courtship flight.
So this process continues, and once the butterflies find their mates, they reproduce and again starts the process of laying eggs. Some butterflies lay their eggs on the top of leaves and others lay them on the underside. This process continues, and the same butterfly lifecycle is followed for all the species of butterflies.
